Diana Dabby

Electrical Engineer & Musican

Bio-

Diana Dabby is an associate professor of electrical engineering and music and the Music Program Director at Olin College of Engineering. She previously taught at MIT, Tufts and Juilliard, and holds degrees in music and electrical engineering from Vassar, Mills, C.C.N.Y., and MIT. Diana combined music and engineering by devising a chaotic mapping for musical variation for her doctoral research at MIT. As a concert pianist, Diana has performed in Weill (Carnegie) Recital Hall, Merkin Concert Hall, Jordan Hall, Symphony Hall Boston, Tanglewood, and abroad. As a composer, she has written for various ensembles and soloists.

Electrical Engineering-

Is the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. Electrical engineers design and develop new electrical equipment, solve problems and test equipment. They work with all kinds of electronic devices, from the smallest pocket devices to large supercomputers.
